The research
What drives risk-asset bull markets. Brief summary below, full PDF linked.
Open full research PDF

The one-line finding. Liquidity direction is the master signal for risk-asset bull markets, not the Bitcoin halving. When money is expanding and real yields are falling, risk assets tend to run; when liquidity drains, they struggle, whatever the halving calendar says.

Ranking of what matters. By strength of evidence: global liquidity first, real (inflation-adjusted) 10-year yields second, Fed policy stance third. Fed policy mostly works through its effect on the first two, so treat it as an upstream cause rather than the signal itself.

Narratives tested against the numbers. The four-year halving cycle looks more like coincidence than cause, and its effect appears to be shrinking each cycle. Fed pivots and rate cuts have preceded rallies but not reliably enough to stand alone. Institutional events (futures 2017, spot ETF 2024) mattered as flows, not as macro triggers.

Sample-size honesty. Only about four full crypto cycles exist, so most of this is "conditions that coincided," not proven causation. Relationships also changed over time, for example Bitcoin's correlation to the tech/risk complex rose sharply after 2020.

The unresolved anomaly. The 2023-2025 run happened while the Fed was tight and real yields were high. It ran on the ETF, the halving, a banking backstop, and the AI capex boom rather than easy money. That divergence is exactly why a single monetary-conditions gauge fails, and why this dashboard splits into three separate forces.

Reading today (2026). Net unfavorable and late-cycle: rates held near 3.75%, real yields elevated and rising, liquidity mixed, inflation still sticky enough to keep the Fed on hold. The classic rescue playbook has not fired.
